What happens when you initialize a repository? Why do you need to do it?
You need repository for tracking changes been made to files.
How is the staging area different from the working directory and the repository? What value do you think it offers?
Staging area is different because you can easily experiment on data without commits and you can easily reset files from it.
How can you use the staging area to make sure you have one commit per logical change?
You can easily accomplish it by adding or reseting files on staging area.
What are some situation when branches would be helpful in keeping your history organized? How would branches help?
In situtations when you need to have different or updated version of same program it's useful to work on different branches.
How do the diagrams help you visualize the branch structure?
Diagrams help because they allow to easily track commits through tree.
What is the result of merging two branches together? Why do we represent it in the diagram the way we do?
The result of merging two branches together is a branch with commits from both branches. We represent it in the diagram to easily track changes in branch.
What are the pros and cons of Git's automatic merging vs. always doing merges manually?
By doing merges manually you retain complete control of changes, but automatic merging has the advantage because it keeps updated branches regularily.
